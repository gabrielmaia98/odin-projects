Bruschetta

Description

Bruschetta (pronounced brew-SKET-tah) is a classic Italian appetizer that is easy to make at home. Toasted bread is topped with a mixture of olive oil, Parmesan cheese, juicy tomatoes and fresh basil.

This recipe text was adapted from this version.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf French bread, cut into ¼-inch slices
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 8 tomatoes, diced
  • ⅓ cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1 ounce Parmesan cheese, freshly grated
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Brush bread slices on both sides lightly with 1 tablespoon oil and place on large baking sheet. Toast bread until golden, about 5 to 10 minutes, turning halfway through.
  3. With the bread slices still hot, add the cheese, tomatoes, basil, salt and black pepper on top of them.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy!
